## General Information
The **Merovingians** were the first Frankish royal family. Initially a pagan clan controlling only a tiny territory in modern-day Belgium, they rose to prominence under **Clovis I**. Around 509 AD, Clovis unified the fractured region of Gaul, establishing the Franks as the ruling military class over a vast Gallo-Roman majority. To legitimise his brand-new empire, Clovis was baptised into Catholic Christianity and crowned **King of the Franks**.

### Culture and Succession
* **The Long Hair:** Merovingian kings wore their hair long as a symbol of their royal bloodline, divine right, and magical warrior status. To cut a king's hair was to legally strip him of his power.

* **The Germanic Custom:** As was ancient Germanic tribal custom, the Merovingians treated the kingdom as private family property. Upon a king's death, Francia was split equally among his sons. This partible inheritance repeatedly fractured the realm into internal sub-kingdoms (like Neustria and Austrasia) and engulfed the empire in centuries of bloody civil wars.

### The Fall of the Dynasty
Over centuries, these civil wars left the Merovingian kings weak and powerless. In 751 AD, **Pepin the Short** (the chief-of-staff / Mayor of the Palace, and father of Charlemagne) officially deposed the last Merovingian king, cutting his long hair and forcing him into a monastery. 

This coup d'état established the [[Carolingian Dynasty]], marking the rise of the second Frankish royal family. The catastrophic tradition of splitting the map would continue to plague Europe until the [[Capetian Dynasty|Capetians]] permanently killed the custom in 987 AD.
