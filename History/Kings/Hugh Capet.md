#### Rule: 987 - 996
### Hugh the Cloaked
After the death of Louis V, of the Carolingians, Hugh was unanimously elected as King of The Franks, over the original intended inheritance of Louis the Fifth's uncle: Charles, Duke of Lower Lorraine, with the help of the main religious authority of "France" at the time, Archbishop Adalbero of Reims.  

However, [[Capetian Dynasty#Before the start of the Capetian dynasty|because of earlier circumstances]], Hugh only had direct control over his extremely small royal domain. West Francia was extremely fragmented and although crowned as King, he was not entirely treated as such by other princes and dukes.

![[WestFrancia_Hugh_Capet_Map.png|300]]

Opposed the most by Odo I, Count of Blois, supported by the empire in Germany, waged war against Hugh. However, with the help of Dukes of Normandy and Anjou, the war was won. Odo of Blois died of an illness while under siege.

With all other adversaries silenced or too young, Hugh secured his lineage.

Still, a great deal of anti-Capetians saw his "usurpation" as illegitimate, and the recount of his coronation was left at the hands of the like and incorporated it as one of kidnappings and depositions as the original account was lost by Gerbert - his secretary, adviser, Pope and Archbishop of Reims.
