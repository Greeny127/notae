## **General information**
The [[Capetian Dynasty|Capetian dynasty]], known also as the House of France, ruled in France from [[Ancien régime|987 to 1328]]

The dynasty was founded by Hugh Capet and was succeeded by his male-line descendants. From his election, the [[House of Capet]] was in power from 987 to 1328. 

To which then, it's cadet branches, the [[House of Valois]] and the [[House of Bourbons]] ruled until [[French Revolution|1792]].

## **Reign of the Houses of France**
987-1328: [[House of Capet]]
1328-1589: [[House of Valois]]
1589-1792: [[House of Bourbons]]

## **Before the start of the [[Capetian Dynasty|Capetian dynasty]]**
Francia, encompassing much of modern day France, Germany, Austria, Belgium and even parts of northern Italy, was made up of smaller kingdoms ruled over by the Merovingians.

Charlemagne took their place as ruler, crowned as emperor in 800, and ruled Francia from 768 to 814, establishing the Carolingian Dynasty. He was thought of, by some, as the "Father of Europe".

Charles divided his kingdom into *Counties* - noblemen assigned to administer over regions. This however, turned into inheritance rather then assignments, developing into the fractured France the Capetians governed over.

This Francia was later divided into East Francia and West Francia, of which the Western half comprised much of France. This realm was soon taken over by the Robertians, of which [[House of Capet#Hugh Capet|Hugh Capet]] descended from.
